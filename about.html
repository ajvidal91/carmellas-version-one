"Clark Moulaison has been a community pillar for almost 30 years"-Mayor Thomas Menino

Growing up in East Boston amongst 5 brothers, Clark Moulaison got his start at Frank's National Italian market early on in his life. After earning a degree in marketing from Suffolk University, he opened American Ice Cream with his brothers becoming the first entrepreneurs since his family arrived in America in the late 1800s. Once American Ice Cream closed shop after nearly two decades in business, Clark went on to help revitalize local storefronts in Maverick, Central, and Day Square as Director of the Main Street project from 2006-2012. As rewarding as it was to help beautify Eastie with the non-profit East Boston Gateway, Clark still had the desire to still be present in his community as a business owner and role model. So, towards the end of 2012 Clark purchased Gloria Food Market from longtime proprietor Gennario Del Prete. Since then, Clark has presided over the thriving business and has watched the Jefferies Point community evolve. Keeping it in the family hasn't changed, as his namesake wife, and his sons, Anthony and Clark III, all contribute to make Carmella's what it is today. By keeping his children in the business, the Moulaison family presence will continue in East Boston for generations to come.
